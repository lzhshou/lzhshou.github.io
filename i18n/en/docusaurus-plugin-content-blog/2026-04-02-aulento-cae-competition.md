The student team "Jiu Tan" from our research group won the Excellence Award in the first SAM Domestic Structural Analysis Software Application Competition.

<!-- truncate -->

**Team Information:**
- Team Name: Jiu Tan (玖探队)
- Members: Lu Yuzhou, Liu Mingjing, Chen Jianying
- Advisor: Lai Zhengshou
- Institution: School of Civil Engineering, Sun Yat-sen University

**Research Content:**
Their work "Comparative Study of Static and Modal Analysis of Offshore Wind Turbine Structures Using SAM and Abaqus" established a finite element model of an offshore wind turbine including tower, nacelle, hub, and simplified blades. Static and modal analyses were conducted using both SAM and Abaqus for comparison.

![Wind Turbine Model](/img/blog/image2.png)
*Offshore wind turbine geometry model*

Results showed high consistency between the two software across 9 typical working conditions. In static analysis, the relative error of tower top displacement ranged from 2.1% to 3.5%, and the relative error of maximum equivalent stress ranged from 1.8% to 3.2%. In modal analysis, the relative error of natural frequencies for all modes was less than 1.5%. These findings validate the engineering applicability of the domestic CAE software SAM for wind turbine structural analysis.

**Competition Background:**

- **Competition Name**: First SAM Domestic Structural Analysis Software Application Competition (also known as "Aulento Cup" Domestic Industrial CAE Software Innovation Competition)
- **Organizer**: China Shipbuilding Aulento (Beijing) Technology Co., Ltd.
- **Date**: March 2026
- **Format**: Online submission and review
- **Participants**: Universities, research institutes, and enterprises nationwide
- **Scale**: 103 teams registered
- **Awards**: 1 First Prize, 2 Second Prizes, 3 Third Prizes, 14 Excellence Awards

**Objectives**: 
Promote independent development of domestic industrial software, validate SAM software's accuracy and applicability in complex engineering problems, cultivate domestic CAE software application talents, and facilitate wider adoption of domestic structural analysis software in engineering practice.
