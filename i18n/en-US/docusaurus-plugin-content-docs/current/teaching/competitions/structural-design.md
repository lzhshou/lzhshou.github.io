# Structural Design Competition

## Overview

The Structural Design Competition of the School of Civil Engineering is a school-level academic competition aimed at cultivating students' structural design and innovation capabilities, and deepening their understanding of structural mechanics principles.

- **Organizer**: School of Civil Engineering, Sun Yat-sen University
- **Participants**: Undergraduate students of the School of Civil Engineering
- **Format**: Structural design, fabrication, and load testing

---

## 2026 7th Edition: Lele Cart Model — Structural Design and Fabrication

### Theme Introduction

This year's competition drew inspiration from the **Lele cart** of the northern grasslands. With its iconic long shaft structure and distinctive creaking sound, the Lele cart epitomizes the migration and daily life of nomadic peoples, and its craftsmanship has been inscribed on the National Intangible Cultural Heritage list, perfectly embodying the structural philosophy of "mastering weight with lightness, achieving complexity through simplicity."

Teams were required to design and fabricate a Lele cart model that could complete a cargo transportation task on a complex track — including **slopes, curves, speed bumps, and single-span bridges** — under the traction of a powered mini-car. The cargo consisted of glass-bead-filled fabric bags (500g each, with teams independently choosing 20–40 bags). The evaluation criteria included transportation time, task completion rate, and cost efficiency. Model materials were limited to bamboo (strips and rods), posing a comprehensive challenge to the teams' mechanical analysis, multi-objective optimization design, and on-site decision-making abilities.

- **Team**: 凡事不要太绝队
- **Project**: 就是209号
